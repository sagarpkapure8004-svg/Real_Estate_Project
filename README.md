The research paper titled "Machine Learning-Driven Buyer Segmentation and Investment Profiling for Real Estate Market Intelligence" serves as a comprehensive analysis of the real estate sector's transition toward high-fidelity, data-driven market intelligence. By leveraging a robust dataset of 7,305 transactions from Real_Estate_Project.csv, this study utilizes Hierarchical Agglomerative Clustering (HAC) to move beyond binary classifications and identify latent buyer personas such as the Utility-Driven Resident, the Strategic Individual Investor, and the Institutional Entity. The technical methodology involves rigorous data preprocessing, including the normalization of sale prices and the engineering of a "Price per Square Foot" metric to standardize financial comparisons across varied unit categories. A critical finding of the research, derived from the correlation analysis of features such as floor area and satisfaction scores, is that while property size and price maintain a near-perfect correlation of 0.96, buyer satisfaction remains a qualitative variable independent of the total capital outlay, with a negligible correlation of -0.01. Furthermore, the study evaluates the efficacy of referral channels, noting that while 55.6% of leads originate from websites, professional agencies remain the essential bridge for high-value institutional capital, particularly in dominant regional hubs like California. Ultimately, this framework demonstrates how machine learning can automate profiling to provide developers and financial institutions with a scalable advantage in strategic positioning and risk management.
